# BYLAWS
## OCTOPUS ARCHITECTURE, INC.
### A Washington Social Purpose Corporation

**Adopted: [DATE]**

---

## ARTICLE I: OFFICES

### Section 1.1 Principal Office
The principal office of the corporation shall be located at such place within or without the State of Washington as the Board of Directors may designate from time to time.

### Section 1.2 Registered Office
The registered office of the corporation required by the Washington Business Corporation Act shall be maintained at the address set forth in the Articles of Incorporation or as changed from time to time.

---

## ARTICLE II: SHAREHOLDERS

### Section 2.1 Annual Meeting
An annual meeting of shareholders shall be held at such time and date as designated by the Board of Directors for the purpose of electing directors and transacting such other business as may come before the meeting.

### Section 2.2 Special Meetings
Special meetings of shareholders may be called by the Board of Directors, the President, or by shareholders holding at least 10% of outstanding shares.

### Section 2.3 Notice of Meetings
Written notice of each shareholder meeting shall be given not less than ten (10) nor more than sixty (60) days before the meeting date to each shareholder entitled to vote.

### Section 2.4 Quorum
A majority of outstanding shares entitled to vote shall constitute a quorum.

### Section 2.5 Voting
Each outstanding share shall be entitled to one vote on each matter submitted to shareholders.

### Section 2.6 Social Purpose Votes
Amendments to the social purposes (Article III of Articles of Incorporation) require approval by two-thirds (2/3) of outstanding shares.

---

## ARTICLE III: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 3.1 Powers
The business and affairs of the corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Directors.

### Section 3.2 Number and Qualifications
The Board shall consist of not less than three (3) nor more than nine (9) directors. The exact number shall be fixed by resolution of the Board.

### Section 3.3 Social Purpose Considerations
In discharging their duties, directors shall consider:
- The interests of shareholders
- The interests of stakeholders materially affected by the corporation
- The social purposes set forth in the Articles of Incorporation
- Short-term and long-term interests of the corporation
- Environmental and community impact

### Section 3.4 Social Purpose Committee
The Board may establish a Social Purpose Committee to:
- Monitor progress on social purpose goals
- Review community contribution reports
- Recommend social purpose initiatives
- Prepare annual social purpose reports

### Section 3.5 Term
Directors shall serve for terms of one (1) year or until their successors are elected and qualified.

### Section 3.6 Meetings
- **Regular Meetings:** Quarterly or as determined by the Board
- **Special Meetings:** May be called by the Chair or any two directors
- **Notice:** 48 hours for special meetings; none required for regular meetings with fixed schedule
- **Quorum:** Majority of directors
- **Action:** Majority of directors present at a meeting with quorum

### Section 3.7 Removal
Directors may be removed with or without cause by vote of shareholders holding a majority of outstanding shares.

---

## ARTICLE IV: OFFICERS

### Section 4.1 Officers
The officers of the corporation shall include:
- Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Chief Technology Officer (CTO)
- Secretary
- Such other officers as the Board may appoint

### Section 4.2 CEO
The CEO shall:
- Be the principal executive officer
- Supervise the business of the corporation
- Execute contracts and documents on behalf of the corporation
- Report to the Board on social purpose progress

### Section 4.3 CFO
The CFO shall:
- Oversee financial operations
- Maintain financial records
- Prepare financial reports
- Ensure compliance with tax and regulatory requirements

### Section 4.4 Secretary
The Secretary shall:
- Maintain corporate records
- Provide notice of meetings
- Record minutes of meetings
- Maintain stock records

---

## ARTICLE V: SOCIAL PURPOSE PROVISIONS

### Section 5.1 Social Purpose Statement
The corporation is committed to pursuing its social purposes as set forth in Article III of the Articles of Incorporation while also seeking to generate reasonable returns for shareholders.

### Section 5.2 Provider Contribution Requirement
All service providers participating in the corporation's marketplace must:
- Contribute minimum 12 hours annually to approved social purpose projects
- Document contributions through the platform
- Maintain contribution compliance for active membership

Approved projects include:
- Graffiti removal and community beautification
- Community murals and public art
- Housing support services
- Food bank assistance
- Translation and language services
- Digital literacy training

### Section 5.3 Annual Social Purpose Report
The corporation shall prepare an annual report that includes:
- Description of social purpose activities
- Metrics on community impact
- Provider contribution hours
- Progress toward social purpose goals
- Financial impact on stakeholders
- Environmental impact metrics

This report shall be:
- Provided to all shareholders
- Made publicly available on the corporation's website
- Filed with appropriate regulatory bodies

### Section 5.4 Impact Metrics
The corporation shall track and report:
- Community service hours facilitated
- Number of individuals/families served
- Economic value generated for local businesses
- Trust score improvements in marketplace
- Environmental benefits achieved

### Section 5.5 Beneficiary Engagement
The corporation shall:
- Engage with communities materially affected by its operations
- Solicit feedback from service providers and customers
- Consider stakeholder input in strategic decisions
- Report on stakeholder satisfaction

---

## ARTICLE VI: STOCK

### Section 6.1 Certificates
Shares may be certificated or uncertificated as determined by the Board.

### Section 6.2 Transfer
Shares shall be transferable on the books of the corporation subject to:
- Applicable securities laws
- Any transfer restrictions in shareholder agreements
- Board approval for transfers exceeding 5% of outstanding shares (to maintain social purpose alignment)

### Section 6.3 Social Purpose Disclosure
Before acquiring shares, purchasers shall be provided:
- Copy of Articles of Incorporation (including social purposes)
- Most recent social purpose report
- Description of social purpose commitments

---

## ARTICLE VII: FISCAL YEAR

The fiscal year of the corporation shall be the calendar year unless changed by resolution of the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: AMENDMENTS

These Bylaws may be amended by:
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors, or
- Majority vote of shareholders

Amendments affecting social purpose provisions (Article V) require:
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board, AND
- Two-thirds (2/3) vote of shareholders

---

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION

The corporation shall indemnify directors, officers, employees, and agents to the fullest extent permitted by Washington law (RCW 23B.08.500 et seq.).

---

## ARTICLE X: DISSOLUTION

Upon dissolution, after paying or providing for debts and obligations, assets shall be distributed:
1. To shareholders as required by law, and
2. Any remaining assets may be transferred to organizations pursuing similar social purposes, as determined by the Board with shareholder approval
