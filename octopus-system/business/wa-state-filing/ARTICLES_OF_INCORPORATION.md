## ARTICLE III: PURPOSE

### 3.1 General Business Purpose

This corporation is organized for the purpose of engaging in any lawful business for which corporations may be organized under the Washington Business Corporation Act.

### 3.2 Social Purpose Corporation Designation

This corporation is organized as a **Social Purpose Corporation** pursuant to RCW 23B.25 and shall operate in a manner that produces a positive effect for one or more categories of persons, entities, communities, or interests, in addition to shareholders.

### 3.3 Specific Social Purposes

The social purposes of this corporation are:

1. **Community Service & Revitalization**
   - Facilitate graffiti removal and community beautification projects
   - Support community mural creation and public art initiatives
   - Provide housing support services to underserved populations
   - Assist food banks and hunger relief organizations

2. **Digital Inclusion & Language Access**
   - Provide translation and language support services
   - Offer digital literacy training to underserved communities
   - Bridge the digital divide for small businesses

3. **Economic Opportunity**
   - Create economic opportunities for verified local service providers
   - Promote fair, transparent, and performance-based business relationships
   - Build trust infrastructure for the emerging AI agent economy

4. **Environmental Stewardship**
   - Prioritize environmentally responsible service providers
   - Support sustainable business practices
   - Reduce waste through efficient marketplace matching

### 3.4 Mandatory Contribution Requirement

All member businesses participating in this corporation's marketplace shall contribute a minimum of twelve (12) hours annually to approved social purpose projects as defined in Section 3.3.

### 3.5 Measurement and Reporting

The corporation shall measure and report on its social purpose impact annually, including:
- Hours of community service facilitated
- Number of community members served
- Economic value generated for local businesses
- Environmental impact metrics
- Trust and safety improvements in online marketplaces

### 7.2 Director Duties Regarding Social Purpose

Pursuant to RCW 23B.25.040, the board of directors shall manage the business and affairs of the corporation in a manner that balances:

1. The pecuniary interests of shareholders
2. The best interests of those materially affected by the corporation's conduct
3. The specific social purposes identified in Article III

Directors may consider the effects of any action upon:
- Employees and workforce
- Subsidiaries and suppliers
- Customers and community members
- Local and global environment
- Long-term and short-term interests of the corporation
- The specific social purposes of the corporation

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENT

These Articles of Incorporation may be amended in accordance with the Washington Business Corporation Act, provided that any amendment to Article III (Social Purposes) requires:

1. Approval by two-thirds (2/3) of the Board of Directors, and
2. Approval by two-thirds (2/3) of outstanding shares entitled to vote

---

## ARTICLE X: SOCIAL PURPOSE CORPORATION PROVISIONS

### 10.1 Annual Report

Pursuant to RCW 23B.25.050, the corporation shall provide to shareholders an annual report describing the efforts of the corporation to pursue its social purposes during the preceding fiscal year.

### 10.2 Change of Control

Any transaction that would result in a change of control of the corporation requires:

1. Approval by two-thirds (2/3) of the Board of Directors, and
2. Approval by two-thirds (2/3) of outstanding shares entitled to vote

The Board must consider the impact on the corporation's social purposes in evaluating any proposed change of control.

### 10.3 Dissolution

Upon dissolution of the corporation, after payment of debts and obligations, remaining assets may be distributed to one or more organizations that pursue similar social purposes, as determined by the Board of Directors.
